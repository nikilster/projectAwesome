Visualization
One way to view Project Awesome as a collection of vision boards.
Why does it work?
It works because what you are essentially doing is focusing your subconscious mind on what you want in life, which will direct your actions to accomplish these things, without you even realizing it. This is a very powerful technique called Visualization used by the most successful people in the world.

However this is still a very well kept secret. Our mission is to spread the knowledege and improve your life.

To understand the psychology behind this technique, keep on reading.
Who does it?
Oprah, Bill Gates, Tiger Woods, Arnold Schwarzenegger, Anthony Robbins, Jack Canfield, Will Smith, Jim Carey, Bruce Lee, and many many other succesful people.
What is a visualization?
A vision is a person, thing, or concept that appears vivid and credible to your mind. It symbolizes something which will or may come to be. When chosen properly, a vision resonates with you to your core. It encompasses something you truly value and want within your life.
Visualization is the process of using one's mind to to vividly picture visions of our own choosing. By putting and keeping visions at the forefront of one's minds, we focus our attention and energy on what really matters to us in life. With the right visions, the visualization becomes extremely powerful. It helps us keep our values and goals in mind, sets the direction for our actions, and determines where we are moving in life.
Example: Jim Carey's $10M check
When he was trying to break into the acting world in 1987, Jim Carey wrote himself a check for $10,000,000 for "acting services rendered" and dated it to Thanksgiving 1995. He carried it around in his wallet and looked at it every day for 8 years. Two months before Thanksgiving in 1995 he received $10,000,000 for his role in Dumb and Dumber.
Hear about it straight from Jim Carey in the video!

The importance of visions
What the mind of man can conceive and believe, it can achieve.
- Napoleon Hill
In 1908, Andrew Carnegie, the steel magnate who built Carnegie Steel Company, commissioned Napoleon Hill to study success. Carnegie believed that the process of success could be outlined into a simple formula, accessible to anyone. He asked Napoleon Hill to study over 500 men and woman, and publish a Philosophy of Personal Achievement.
Nearly three decades later, Napoleon Hill published the landmark book Think and Grow Rich on this Philosophy of Personal Achievement. After 70 years, it is still a perennial best-seller, and is often heralded as the original personal development book. It has inspired man modern icons, such as Tony Robbins, as well as been the seed of many modern personal development books, including the 2006 best-selling self-help book The Secret, by Rhonda Byrne.
Definite Major Purpose. Hill presents the concept of a Definite Major Purpose and tells readers to be clear on an important questions: in what do I truly believe? According to Hill, 98% of people have few or no firm beliefs, and this along puts success out of reach.
Creating a Burning Desire. Once one has developed a definite purpose, and is clear on it, it is necessary to cultivate a burning desire at achieve the purpose. This involves writing down your purpose, and looking at it multiple times a day. It involves keeping the purpose at the top of your mind so that you are constantly planning and taking steps towards your major purpose.
Visions and visualization fit exactly with developing a Definite Major Purpose, and creating a burning desire. When chosen properly, your visions are your major purpose. They resonate with you, and symbolize exactly who you want to be in the future. Visualization keeps the visions at the top of your mind, and help you cultive a burning desire. Ultimately, taking action is up to you, but the tools of visions and the process of visualization are incredibly powerful.
Here is a free copy of this original classic, generously provided by the Australian Soil and Health online library, as well as an interesting video of Napoleon Hill on personal success.
Example: Bruce Lee's Definite Chief Aim.
Before he became an international icon and super star, Bruce Lee wrote himself a letter detailing his definite chief aim.
The text: I, Bruce Lee, will be the first highest paid Oriental super star in the United States. In return I will give the most exciting performances and render the best of quality in the capacity of an actor. Starting 1970 I will achieve world fame and from then onward till the end of 1980 I will have in my possession $10,000,000. I will live the way I please and achieve inner harmony and happiness.
He acted upon this burning desire and followed up with five classic films in the next decade: The Big Boss (1971), First of Fury (1972), Way of the Dragon (1972), Enter the Dragon (1973), and The Game of Death (1978). He shot to superstardom and is now one of the iconic legends in martial arts and in film.

What is a vision board?
A vision board is a useful tool for visualization. It is simply a board of visions used to remind oneself of what matters in life. People typically put vision boards in prominent locations, such as their bedroom or office space, to constantly remind themselves about their visions.
An example vision board, made of magazine cutouts.
Photo credit: WiseWellWoman @ Flickr
Building a vision board is all about defining your Major Definite Purpose. By choosing the visions to place on your board, you are explicitly putting down who you want to be, and where you want your life to look like in the future.
On of our major goals at Project Awesome is to make it easy to create meaningful personal vision boards. If you don't know what you want, feel free to browse other vision boards. You will begin to get an idea of what resonates with you and what doesn't. Take your time building your board, and understand that it might change over time. The important thing is that you continually work at it, and make your visions perfectly clear to yourself.
Sharing your visions.
One of the other goals is to help people share visions. There are many benefts to putting your visions out there for the world to see.
Makes your visions real. First, the act of sharing makes your intentions real. It means that you publicly state what you are about, who you want to be, and where you want to go.
Inspiration. Second, you may inspire others. As Napoleon Hill states, 98% of people are not clear on their goals. By sharing your own, you provide examples to other people about visions that resonate with you. This means that others can repost your visions and incorporate them into their own hopes and dreams.
Interaction and support. Third, it allows for interaction with the rest of the world. Most likely, there are many others that share the visions as you. We hope to build a community where people can share and support each other in the journey towards their visions.
Some visions, often the best visions, are so big, so audacious, that they scare us to think about. They make us question ourselves, and what we are made out of. If you are comfortable sharing them, great! But there are visions that seem fragile, and for those, we allow people to mark visions as private. Simply create or edit your vision to be private, and you can keep it to yourself.
Cultivating your visions.
Building your vision board is an important step. But beyond that, it is important to keep your visions at the top of our mind, and create a burning desire to attain them. We support this is several ways.
Daily reminders and inspiration. Each morning, we send out an email with a random vision on your board, and other inspirational content. These provide a perfect time and place to reflect upon your visions, and plan out your day.
Social support and interaction. As mentioned above, we hope to build a strong supportive community. Don't be shy about reposting and commenting on other vision boards. Remember, every comment draws the original user to come back, think about, and then interact with their vision. By fostering a strong community, people will be helping others keep their visions at the top of their minds.. an important step to cultivating that burning desire to achieve their visions!
If you haven't already, build yourself a vision board and join our community!
